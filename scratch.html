Introduction to Cats

Cats were first domesticated roughly 10,000 years ago in the Near East, where wildcats were drawn to early agricultural settlements by rodent populations. Over millennia, selective breeding and natural adaptation produced the extraordinary diversity of breeds, coat types, and temperaments we know today.

Unlike dogs, whose domestication dramatically reshaped their anatomy and behavior, cats retain many traits of their wild ancestors, making them one of the most complex and independent domestic animals.

How Are Cats Classified?

Cat breeds are organized in several ways. Below are the major classification frameworks used by notable registries.

  1. Coat Type
    • Short-haired — the most common; low maintenance grooming
    • Long-haired — flowing coats requiring daily brushing
    • Hairless — little to no fur; require skin care and warmth
    • Curly — genetically wavy or kinked fur
    • Wire-haired — stiff, crimped coat texture
  2. Body Type
    • Cobby — compact, round, heavily boned
    • Foreign/Oriental — slender, angular, long-limbed
    • Long & Substantial — large, muscular, long body
  3. Geographic Origin
    • Western breeds — developed primarily in Europe and North America
    • Eastern & South Asian breeds — originating in Asia
    • Middle Eastern & African breeds — closest to wildcat lineage
    • Natural breeds — evolved with minimal human selection
    • Hybrid breeds — crosses with wild relatives
  4. Classified by Temperament & Activity Level
    • High-energy — vocal and active; need stimulation
    • Calm — gentle and affectionate
    • Independentd — prefer their own space
    • Dog-like — follow owners, enjoy company

The World's Most Popular Breeds

While over 70 breeds are formally recognized, a handful dominate homes and registries worldwide. Here are the most popular, with what makes each one distinctive.

Domestic Shorthair
Mixed Breed

The "mutt" of the cat world and by far the most common. Their genetic diversity makes them hardy, adaptable, and unique in personality.

Persian
Developed Breed

Prized for their long coat and flat face. Persians are calm, quiet, and prefer a similar environment.

Maine Coon
Natural Breed

The largest domestic cat breed. "Dog-like" in personality. Playful, loyal, and sociable. Tufted ears and bushy tail are hallmarks.

Siamese
Natural Breed

Known for their striking blue eyes, coloring, and vocal personality.

Sphynx
Mutation Breed

Hairless and wrinkled. They are energetic, warm, and people-oriented. They are not hypoallergenic.

Bengal
Hybrid Breed

Developed from crosses with the Asian Leopard Cat. Wild spots, high energy, and an athletic build. Not for first-time owners.
